Robust-TO: Trustworthy Temporal Reasoning under Visual Disturbances
Supplementary Material — Visual Demonstrations of the Robust-TO Pipeline

Abstract — Standard Video-LLMs exhibit brittle failures when confronted with concurrent real-world distortions (motion blur, glare, occlusion). We introduce Robust-TO, a three‑stage pipeline that explicitly models per‑frame quality, disturbance‑aware tool routing, and confidence‑weighted evidence synthesis. The following figures illustrate the core mechanisms: per‑frame quality profiling & trustworthy frame selection, disturbance‑aware tool routing, and confidence‑weighted three‑tier evidence synthesis. Compared to naive Video-LLM baselines, Robust-TO reliably answers queries even under severe degradation (see Figure 3). Figure 1 | System Architecture Overview
Per‑frame quality profiling & trustworthy frame selection → disturbance‑aware tool routing
Illustration (a). The Robust-TO pipeline integrates three sequential modules: (i) Quality Profiler estimates blur, brightness, and occlusion per frame; (ii) Disturbance‑Aware Router selectively invokes specialized tools (e.g., deblurring, enhancement) based on predicted degradation; (iii) Confidence‑Weighted Synthesizer fuses multi‑source evidence into a final answer. Gray arrows indicate fallback paths when confidence is low.

Figure 2 | Disturbance‑Aware Frame Selection
Suppressing corrupted frames before any high‑level perception
Illustration (b). For each frame, Robust-TO computes a quality vector (Qblur, Qbright, Qoccl). Frames that fall below an adaptive threshold are omitted from the evidence pool, preventing noisy input from contaminating reasoning. The schematic shows original video frames (top) vs. selected trustworthy frames (bottom) — frames with heavy motion blur or glare are discarded, preserving only reliable visual tokens.

Figure 3 | Confidence‑Weighted Synthesis & Benchmark Victory
Three‑Tier Evidence Synthesis & Robustness Gain
Illustration (c). All extracted facts are grouped into HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW confidence tiers. Conflicting low‑confidence claims are automatically discarded when they contradict high‑confidence observations. The right panel shows benchmark comparisons: on the Distorted-VideoQA suite (concurrent blur + glare + occlusion), standard Video‑LLMs (e.g., VideoLLaMA, LAVILA) achieve ≤ 34% accuracy, whereas Robust‑TO attains 78.6% accuracy and robust answer consistency. Bottom example: given a query “What color is the ball after the occlusion?”, only Robust‑TO correctly outputs “red”; baselines hallucinate “blue” or “unknown”.
