The Blind Trust Problem

Video‑LLMs silently fail under real‑world corruptions

Under motion blur, glare, or occlusion, frontier Video-LLMs lose 15–30 accuracy points on embodied benchmarks while self‑reported confidence remains unchanged – we formalize this as the Blind Trust Problem.

Robust‑TO is an agentic framework that integrates per‑frame trustworthiness into every stage of reasoning. A unified (result, confidence) interface couples tool certainty with parameter‑free disturbance estimates, enabling reliability‑guided frame selection, disturbance‑aware tool routing, and three‑tier evidence synthesis. Trained with a confidence‑cost GRPO reward, Robust‑TO achieves +10.6 avg accuracy over open‑source baselines, with <5% latency overhead on clean inputs and the smallest clean‑to‑corrupted drop among all compared methods.

Core mechanisms

Three pillars of robust reasoning

🧩

Disturbance‑aware evidence acquisition

Frames ranked by reliability × relevance, sub‑queries routed to tools robust to dominant corruption (blur → captioning, occlusion → action recognition).

📐

Confidence‑weighted synthesis

Three reliability tiers: HIGH drives conclusion, MEDIUM checked for consistency, LOW fallback with explicit uncertainty.

🎯

Confidence‑cost GRPO

Joint optimization of correctness, evidence reliability, and efficiency. Frozen N* estimator prevents reward gaming.

🔌

Plug‑and‑play tools

detect_objects, caption_frame, track_temporal, recognize_action, read_text – each with unified (result, confidence) contract.

Experimental validation

State‑of‑the‑art robustness & accuracy

Evaluated on UrbanVideo-Bench (outdoor embodied tasks: LP, CF, PE, AG) and VSI-Bench (indoor spatial: RDist, RDir, RP, AO) under clean and RoVA‑V1 corrupted variants (Motion Blur, Glare, Occlusion, Low‑Light, Gaussian Noise).
